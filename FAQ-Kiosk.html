Welcome to the FAQ Page

Here, you will find commonly asked questions and the answers to them.

My Username?

Your username is assigned to you when you are admitted as a student. It will be used to access your TTU email account (Office365 TTU email, online library databases, campus lab computers, iLearn, and wireless access).

If you do not know your username, go to Eagle Online and under Personal Information - View E-mail Addresses and Username you should see your TTU Username listed first.

Help resetting my password?

To access your campus email account you must first reset the password.

To reset your password using this kiosk, select the Home button on the navigation bar below. Then choose the button for Reset Your Password. This will take you to the Password Self-Service Portal (ssp.tntech.edu).

My Email?
  • You can get to the student email by going to the TTU homepage and selecting the Students link at the top of the page.
  • Select Email in the drop-down.
  • You can also get there by typing in outlook.com/students.tntech.edu

How do I forward emails from my Student Email to another Email address?

  • Log into your student email account.
  • In the top-right corner, select the gear icon.
  • The Settings panel will slide out from the right. Click in the Search all settings textbox and type "forwarding".
  • Select the Forwarding link and the Forwarding Settings will appear on the left.
  • Select Start forwarding and enter the email address you would like to receive your TTU emails. If you would like to keep a copy of your TTU messages in the TTU email inbox as well (this is recommended), place a checkmark beside Keep a copy of forwarded messages.
  • If in the future you would like to disable forwarding, return to these settings and instead select Stop forwarding.
How to connect my smart phone or tablet to my TTU email?
  • Login to your student email account.
  • In the top-right corner, select the gear icon.
  • The Settings panel will slide out from the right. Click in the Search all settings textbox and type "mobile".
  • Select the Mobile devices link and the Mobile device settings will appear on the left.
  • In the descriptive text under the title, look to the end for the Learn more link. Click it for help documentation in phone and tablet setup.
What software and hardware is available to purchase for my personal computer?

Students can purchase computers from Dell and Apple. There are also several software packages that are available either through TTU contracts or other companies with student pricing. To view a list of the hardware and software purchase options available for students, go to www.tntech.edu/its/itspurchases/softwareathome. Alternatively, you can go to the ITS website and select the Technology Purchases link on the left. Then select Pricing for Home Use.

How to access OneDrive for storing files?

Through TTU you have access to OneDrive for storing and accessing files from almost anywhere. You can access your OneDrive files by logging into your student email. Look to the app icon in the top-left. OneDrive is one of the options in tech app list. Select it to access your OneDrive storage. There is a 5GB limit to this storage.

How to connect to the internet from my dorm room (or Tech Village)?

Wireless internet access is available across campus by registering with the EagleNet Wireless Service, which allows you to obtain a personal access key to authenticate any of your wireless devices. To access the EagleNet Registration Portal on this kiosk, select the Home button on the navigation bar below. Then choose the button for Renew Your EagleNet Access Key.

In order to connect using a wired connection, you must register through the Bradford Network Access Control. For instructions in this process, please consult www.tntech.edu/its/computing/bradford-agent.

My iLearn courses?
  • Go to http://ilearn.tntech.edu to access your courses.
  • For online support resources go to http://www.tntech.edu/ilearn.
  • Report any other issues to the myTECH Helpdesk.

Student accounts are added to the system prior to the beginning of the semester but you will not be able to see your courses until the first day of the semester. All courses are not taught using iLearn so you need to check with your instructor to confirm that you will be using it for your courses. If the course section is M10, 500 or R50 (R50 courses must be accessed by going to the Regents Online Degree Program (RODP) server), it is designated as a completely online course and should be accessible through iLearn (or the RODP server).

To login, use your TTU username and password (be sure you have already set up your TTU email and accessed it at least once).

How to download Respondus Lockdown Browser?

Some courses require Respondus Lockdown Browser to complete exams and quizzes through iLearn.

To download the Respondus Lockdown Browser, visit www.tntech.edu/institute/services/respondus and follow the instructions there.

NOTE: The Respondus Lockdown Browser is not a browser plug-in, but a program unto itself.

Computer workstations?

TTU provides students with several computer labs across campus. The locations that are open for general use are the Library Learning Commons lab, Clement Hall 313A, Johnson Hall 214B and 214C, and Henderson Hall 111 (only available for open use after 5pm). The Clement Hall, Johnson Hall, and Henderson Hall labs are subject to be reserved for classroom use, so if the room is in use for a class, please refrain from entering the lab unless you are enrolled in that class. To view information about lab locations, their hours, and more, visit www.tntech.edu/its/computing.

A place to print my files?

TTU provides students with several computer labs across campus. The locations that are open for general use are the Library Learning Commons lab, Clement Hall 313A, Johnson Hall 214B and 214C, and Henderson Hall 111 (only available for open use after 5pm). Each lab features at least one Pharos-enabled black-and-white printer. For color printing and advanced finishing options (hole punching, stapling), print in the Library Learning Commons. The Clement Hall, Johnson Hall, and Henderson Hall labs are subject to be reserved for classroom use, so if the room is in use for a class, please refrain from entering the lab unless you are enrolled in that class. To view information about lab locations, their hours, and more, visit www.tntech.edu/its/computing.

If you encounter difficulty in printing, such as an error message on the Pharos print release station or the printer does not print your documents, please contact the Learning Commons Circulation Desk or the myTECH Helpdesk.

Loaning equipment?

Students can checkout equipment from the Circulation Desk in the Library Learning Commons. The equipment available for checkout is viewable on the Library website.

The Learning Resource Center (LRC) in the College of Education has other equipment available for student checkout. Some of this equipment is limited to College of Education students only. View the checkout policies and available items at www.tntech.edu/education/lrc.

Wireless access?

Wireless internet access is available to students, faculty, staff, and visitors to campus. Students, faculty, and staff must register for an EagleNet Access Key in order to gain access. To access the EagleNet Registration Portal on this kiosk, select the Home button on the navigation bar below. Then choose the button for Renew Your EagleNet Access Key.

Alternatively, you may visit connect.tntech.edu.

Help accessing my Eagle Online information (account balances, registration, grades, schedule, financial aid)?

You can access Eagle Online by going to www.tntech.edu/eo. Select Enter Eagle Online and then enter your User ID (T-Number) and Eagle Online PIN. If you've forgotten your Eagle Online PIN, you may select the Forgot PIN? button or contact the myTECH Helpdesk.
