Welcome to the FAQ Page

Here, you will find commonly asked questions and the answers to them.

My Username?

Your username is assigned to you when you are officially employed by the University. It will be used to access your TTU email account (Office365 TTU email, online library databases, campus lab computers, iLearn, and wireless access).

If you do not know your username, go to Eagle Online and under Personal Information - View E-mail Addresses and Username you should see your TTU Username listed first.

Help resetting my password?

To access your campus email account you must first reset the password.

To reset your password using this kiosk, select the Home button on the navigation bar below. Then choose the button for Reset Your Password. This will take you to the Password Self-Service Portal (ssp.tntech.edu).

My Email?

Faculty will have access to their email through their campus computer using Microsoft Outlook.

  • You can also get to your email by going to the TTU homepage and selecting the Faculty & Staff tab at the top of the page.
  • Alternatively, you may go directly to office365.tntech.edu.

If you run into difficulties, you may contact the myTECH Helpdesk.

How to connect my smart phone or tablet to my TTU email?
  • Login to your TTU email account.
  • In the top-right corner, select the gear icon.
  • The Settings panel will slide out from the right. Click in the Search all settings textbox and type "mobile".
  • Select the Mobile devices link and the Mobile device settings will appear on the left.
  • In the descriptive text under the title, look to the end for the Learn more link. Click it for help documentation in phone and tablet setup.
How to send an email to my class?

Two weeks prior to the start of each semester instructors of a course can send an email to their class roster by using the email distribution lists provided by ITS or at any time from within Banner. To find out more go to the ITS website and look for the Email link on the left. Hit the down arrow and then select the down arrow beside Faculty/Staff Email Information. Then choose the link titled Email Class Lists or Majors. and select under Email the Emailing a Class Section or Major link.

NOTE: This only applies to Faculty.

How to purchase software for my office computer?

Go to the Technology Purchases link. The instructions are listed. You can also see what Hardware and Software contracts we have available as well as the recommended computer bundles for campus. For consultation, contact your ACT liaison.

What software and hardware is available to purchase for my personal computer?

Faculty can purchase computers from Dell and Apple. There are also several software packages that are available either through TTU contracts or other companies with employee pricing.

For more information look at the Technology Purchases section of the ITS web site. Select Pricing for Home Use to view more information.

How to get Respondus products?

Faculty can download Respondus products (this includes Respondus quiz creator, StudyMate, Monitor and Lockdown Browser) from on-campus only. You can also get to the information using the menus on our site by selecting ITS Forms & Downloads and selecting the Respondus Software link under Instructional/Faculty Requests.

How to access student information for advising?

You will need to login to Self-Service Banner (SSB). Select the Banner Logins link and then select Login to Banner SSB. Select Enter Secure Banner Web Self Service. You will use your T-number for the User ID. Once you have logged in, choose the Faculty Services tab.

You can look up student information for those whom you advise, enter midterm and final grades, attendance reporting, class lists and more.

How to access my employee information?

You will need to login to Self-Service Banner (SSB). Select the Banner Logins link and then select Login to Banner SSB. Select Enter Secure Banner Web Self Service. You will use your T-number for the User ID. Once you have logged in, choose the Employee tab. To view or change personal information select the Personal Information tab.

How to reserve a lab for class?

Go to the Computing/Labs link. Under PC Lab Reservations on the page you can View lab reservations or Reserve a lab for the current semester.

How to request software to be installed in the lab?

Go to the Computing/Labs link. Under Request Software in Labs complete the request form. Make sure to note the deadline dates for each semester.

NOTE: You can also view this form from the Online Forms & Downloads page on the ITS site.

How to get web page space on the Instructional Web (IWeb) server?

Go to the ITS Forms & Downloads link. Under the Web Directory Accounts complete the Faculty Web Site Request Form. Once the directory is setup you will receive an email letting you know how to gain access.

How to get courses added to iLearn?

First, you must have attended the Introduction to iLearn training workshop. You can view the current available workshops online. Workshops are offered through the Technology Institute. Once you have done this, you will need to have your departmental secretary mark the course(s) in Banner. After the course is marked in Banner the course(s) and class list(s) will populate iLearn automatically.
If you do not see the course(s) within a day, please call 931-372-3675 or email institute@tntech.edu.

Wireless access?

All students, faculty and staff have access to the campus wireless service. Faculty and staff will use their TTU domain account (the same account as your email) to access the wireless points. For more information select Computing/Labs and then Wireless Access.

Loaning equipment?

ITS has some equipment available for short-term checkout. Call at 931-372-3595 or email whoffert@tntech.edu. Items for checkout include:

  • laptops (both Windows and Mac)
  • projectors
  • portable projector screens

The Technology Institute has equipment available for short-term checkout. The standard check-out time is two weeks. Call =931-372-3675 or email institute.tntech.edu the for more information. Items for checkout include:

  • tablets (HP and Fujitsu)
  • video cameras
  • still cameras
  • web cams
  • microphones
  • tripods
  • projectors.

The Learning Resource Center in the College of Education has other equipment available for checkout. View the checkout policies and available items online.
